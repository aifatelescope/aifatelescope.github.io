Comments:

Double shadow transit, with Ganymede and Io, recored for two hours. Jupiter was about 60 degrees above the horizon. All images are based on data from a DMK21AF04.AS monochrome 640x480 8-bit camera, at the Cassegrain focus of the AIfA telescope.

In the animation, each frame is a processed stack of the best 100 (out of about 1000) images. Recording interval towards the end of the sequence is one movie per minute. Click here for a cleaner mp4 version.

Second image is a comparision between a Baader UV-IR rejection filter (i.e., broad V band, left) and an Astronomik Planet IR Pro 742 (i.e., near IR, right).
